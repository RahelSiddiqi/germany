## üìã Structure Template

### Paragraph 1: Introduction (2 sentences)

**Paraphrase the question** - DO NOT copy!

> The [graph/chart/diagram] shows/illustrates/compares/presents [what] + [where/who] + [when].

**Example:**

> Question: "The bar chart shows the percentage of people who went to the cinema once a month or more in five countries in 2010."
>
> Paraphrase: "The bar chart illustrates cinema attendance rates in five nations during 2010, specifically the proportion of the population visiting cinemas at least monthly."

### Paragraph 2: Overview (2-3 sentences)

**Main trends/features - NO numbers here!**

Start with:

-   "Overall, ..."
-   "In general, ..."
-   "It is clear that..."

**Include:**

-   Highest/lowest
-   Main trend (increase/decrease)
-   Key comparison

**Example:**

> "Overall, it is evident that cinema attendance was highest in the USA and lowest in Japan. Additionally, Western countries generally showed higher rates than Asian nations."

**First group of data with specific numbers**

## üìà Line Graph Language

### Describing Movement

| Direction      | Verbs                                   | Nouns                         |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------- | ----------------------------- |
| ‚Üë Up           | rise, increase, grow, climb, go up      | a rise, an increase, growth   |
| ‚Üì Down         | fall, decrease, decline, drop, go down  | a fall, a decrease, a decline |
| ‚Üí Flat         | remain stable, stay constant, level off | stability, no change          |
| ‚Üë‚Üì Up and down | fluctuate, vary                         | fluctuation, variation        |
| Peak           | reach a peak, peak at                   | a peak                        |
| Bottom         | bottom out, hit a low                   | a low point                   |

### Speed & Degree

| Degree | Adverbs                                                  | Adjectives                         |
| ------ | -------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Big    | dramatically, significantly, substantially, considerably | dramatic, significant, substantial |
| Medium | moderately, noticeably                                   | moderate, noticeable               |
| Small  | slightly, marginally, minimally                          | slight, marginal, minimal          |

| Speed | Adverbs                   | Adjectives          |
| ----- | ------------------------- | ------------------- |
| Fast  | sharply, rapidly, steeply | sharp, rapid, steep |
| Slow  | gradually, steadily       | gradual, steady     |

### Sentence Patterns

**Pattern 1: Subject + Verb + Adverb**

> Sales **increased dramatically** from 100 to 500.

**Pattern 2: There was + Adjective + Noun**

> **There was a sharp increase** in sales from 100 to 500.

**Pattern 3: Subject + saw/experienced + Noun**

> The company **saw a significant rise** in profits.

## üö´ Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Giving Opinions

‚ùå "I think the increase is because..."
‚úÖ Just describe what you see

### Mistake 2: Copying Question Words

‚ùå "The bar chart shows the percentage of people who went to the cinema..."
‚úÖ Paraphrase: "The bar chart illustrates cinema attendance rates..."

### Mistake 3: No Overview

‚ùå Going straight to details
‚úÖ Always include "Overall,..." paragraph

### Mistake 4: Too Many Numbers

‚ùå Listing every single number

### Mistake 5: No Comparisons

‚ùå Describing each bar separately
‚úÖ Compare and contrast: "While X rose, Y fell"

## üìù Sample Answer Structure

### Line Graph Question:

> "The graph shows coffee and tea consumption in the UK from 1990 to 2020."

### Sample Answer:

**Introduction:**
The line graph illustrates the consumption levels of coffee and tea in the United Kingdom over a 30-year period, from 1990 to 2020.

**Overview:**
Overall, it is evident that coffee consumption increased significantly throughout the period, while tea consumption showed a declining trend. By the end of the period, coffee had become more popular than tea.

**Body 1:**
In 1990, tea was the dominant hot beverage, consumed by approximately 80% of the population, compared to just 30% for coffee. Tea consumption remained relatively stable until 2000, after which it began to decline gradually, falling to around 55% by 2020.

**Body 2:**
In contrast, coffee consumption rose steadily from 30% in 1990 to 50% in 2010. This upward trend accelerated between 2010 and 2020, with the figure reaching approximately 70% by the end of the period. Notably, coffee overtook tea in popularity around 2015.
