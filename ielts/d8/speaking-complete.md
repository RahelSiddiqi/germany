## ðŸ“ Part 1: Sample Questions & Answers

### Topic: HOMETOWN

**Q: Where is your hometown?**

> "My hometown is Dhaka, which is the capital city of Bangladesh. It's a bustling metropolis in the central part of the country, known for its vibrant culture and historical sites."

**Q: What do you like most about your hometown?**

> "What I appreciate most is the food culture. Dhaka has incredible street food, and I particularly love the variety of traditional dishes you can find there. Also, the people are incredibly hospitable."

**Q: Has your hometown changed much in recent years?**

> "Absolutely, it's undergone tremendous transformation. When I was younger, many areas were still undeveloped, but now there are modern shopping malls, metro systems, and high-rise buildings everywhere. It's almost unrecognizable in some parts."

**Q: Do you use technology much in your daily life?**

> "Yes, quite extensively actually. I rely on my smartphone for everything from communication to navigation. I also use my laptop for work and studying. It's become an essential part of my routine."

**Q: What's your favorite gadget?**

> "That would definitely be my smartphone. It's incredibly versatile â€“ I use it for reading news, managing my schedule, staying in touch with friends, and even for photography when I travel. I couldn't imagine life without it."

> "To some extent, yes. My grandparents, for example, initially struggled with smartphones. However, I've noticed that many older people are becoming increasingly tech-savvy. My father, who's in his sixties, now uses video calling regularly to keep in touch with relatives abroad."

### Strategy Recap

2. **2 minutes:** Speak fluently, cover all points
3. **Follow-up:** 1-2 brief questions after

| ----------- | ---------------------------------- |

### Useful Phrases for Part 2

**Opening:**

-   "I'd like to talk about..."
-   "The [topic] I've chosen is..."
-   "I'm going to describe..."

**Adding details:**

-   "What made it special was..."
-   "The thing I remember most is..."
-   "In particular, I was impressed by..."

**Time references:**

-   "This happened about [X] years ago..."
-   "At the time, I was..."
-   "Looking back now..."

**Feelings:**

-   "I felt incredibly [happy/nervous/excited]..."
-   "It made me realize..."
-   "This experience taught me..."

**Concluding:**

-   "Overall, it was..."
-   "That's why I'll always remember..."
-   "It remains one of my most [memorable] experiences."

## ðŸ—£ï¸ PART 3: Two-Way Discussion

### What to Expect

-   Abstract, deeper questions
-   Related to Part 2 topic
-   Requires opinions, analysis, speculation

### Question Types

1. **Opinion:** "What do you think about...?"
2. **Compare:** "How has X changed compared to the past?"
3. **Speculate:** "Do you think X will change in the future?"
4. **Evaluate:** "What are the advantages/disadvantages of...?"
5. **Generalize:** "Why do people...?"

### Response Structure

**Step 1:** Give your opinion
**Step 2:** Explain/elaborate
**Step 3:** Give an example
**Step 4:** Add another perspective (optional)

### Sample Part 3 Exchanges

**Topic:** Technology (from Part 2 about a gadget)

**Q: How has technology changed the way people communicate?**

> "Technology has completely revolutionized communication, I'd say. In the past, people relied on letters and landline phones, which often meant waiting days or even weeks for responses. Now, with instant messaging and video calls, we can connect with anyone in the world in seconds.

> However, I think there's a downside too. Face-to-face interaction has decreased, and some argue that digital communication lacks the depth of in-person conversations. For example, it's easy to misinterpret the tone of a text message because there are no visual cues."

**Q: Do you think technology will make traditional jobs obsolete?**

> "That's an interesting question. I believe some jobs will inevitably disappear â€“ routine tasks that can be automated are already being replaced by machines. For instance, self-checkout machines have reduced the need for cashiers in many supermarkets.

> However, I'm optimistic that new jobs will emerge to replace them. History has shown that technological advances create opportunities as well as challenges. The key will be ensuring that people have access to training and education to adapt to the changing job market."

## ðŸ”‘ Useful Phrases for All Parts

### Giving Opinions

-   "In my view..."
-   "From my perspective..."
-   "I firmly believe that..."
-   "I'm of the opinion that..."

### Expressing Uncertainty

-   "I'm not entirely sure, but..."
-   "It's difficult to say, but I think..."
-   "I would imagine that..."

### Agreeing/Disagreeing (in discussions)

-   "I completely agree that..."
-   "I'm not so sure about that..."
-   "While I see your point, I think..."

### Hedging (avoiding extremes)

-   "Generally speaking..."
-   "In most cases..."
-   "It tends to be..."
-   "More often than not..."

### Giving Examples

-   "For instance..."
-   "A good example of this is..."
-   "To illustrate this point..."
-   "Take [X], for example..."

### Clarifying

-   "What I mean is..."
-   "To put it another way..."
-   "In other words..."

### Concluding a Point

-   "So, that's why I think..."
-   "All things considered..."
-   "Taking all of this into account..."

## ðŸŽ¤ Pronunciation Tips

### Word Stress

-   Emphasize the right syllable
-   PHOTO-graph vs. pho-TOG-ra-phy
-   COM-puter, not com-PU-ter

### Sentence Stress

-   Stress **content words** (nouns, verbs, adjectives)
-   Reduce **function words** (the, a, to, and)
-   "I WENT to the STORE to BUY some FOOD."
