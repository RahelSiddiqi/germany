## üîÑ PROCESS DIAGRAMS

### What They Show

-   Manufacturing processes (how products are made)
-   Natural processes (water cycle, life cycle)
-   Procedures (how something works)

### Key Features

-   Steps happen in a specific order
-   Uses **passive voice** (is made, are sorted)
-   May have cycles (circular) or linear flow

---

## üìã Process Structure

### Paragraph 1: Introduction

Paraphrase what the diagram shows.

> "The diagram illustrates the process of [what] / how [something] is [made/produced]."

### Paragraph 2: Overview

Summarize the main stages (no details yet).

> "Overall, there are [X] main stages in the process, beginning with [first stage] and ending with [final stage]."

### Paragraph 3: Body 1

Describe the first half of stages with details.

### Paragraph 4: Body 2

Describe the remaining stages with details.

## üîë Essential Process Language

### Sequencing Words

| Position  | Words                                                          |
| --------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Start     | First, Initially, At the first stage, To begin with            |
| Middle    | Then, Next, After that, Subsequently, Following this           |
| Same time | Meanwhile, At the same time, Simultaneously, During this stage |
| End       | Finally, Lastly, In the final stage, Eventually                |

### Passive Voice Structures

**Simple passive (most common):**

> "The materials **are collected** from farms."
> "The product **is heated** to 100¬∞C."

**Passive with modal verbs:**

> "The mixture **can be stored** for up to 3 days."
> "The product **must be dried** before packaging."

**Passive with "where/which":**

> "The factory, **where the materials are processed**, is located..."
> "The container, **which is filled with water**, is then heated."

## üìù Sample Process Answer

### Diagram: How chocolate is made

**Introduction:**
The diagram illustrates the manufacturing process of chocolate, from the initial harvesting of cacao beans to the final production of chocolate bars.

**Overview:**
Overall, the process involves ten distinct stages, beginning with the collection of ripe cacao pods and concluding with the packaging of chocolate products. The beans undergo several transformations, including drying, roasting, and mixing with other ingredients.

**Body 1:**
Initially, ripe cacao pods are harvested from trees and then split open to extract the beans. These beans are subsequently spread out to dry in the sun for approximately one to two weeks. Once dried, the beans are packed into sacks and transported to the factory.

**Body 2:**
At the factory, the beans are first roasted at high temperatures, which develops their flavor. Following this, the outer shells are removed, and the inner nibs are crushed to produce cacao liquor. This liquor is then mixed with sugar and milk to create chocolate. Finally, the mixture is poured into molds, cooled, and packaged for distribution to retailers.

## üó∫Ô∏è MAP COMPARISONS

### What They Show

-   Changes to a place over time (1990 vs 2020)
-   Comparison of two locations
-   Development of an area

### Key Features

-   Uses **past tense** for completed changes
-   Uses **present perfect** for changes up to now
-   Compares before and after

---

## üìã Map Structure

### Paragraph 1: Introduction

> "The two maps illustrate [changes to/development of] [place] between [year] and [year]."

### Paragraph 2: Overview

> "Overall, the area has undergone/experienced significant [development/transformation]. The most notable changes include [main change 1] and [main change 2]."

### Paragraph 3: Body 1

Describe one area or first set of changes.

### Paragraph 4: Body 2

Describe another area or remaining changes.

## üîë Essential Map Language

### Describing Changes

| Change Type | Vocabulary                                                  |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Built/added | was constructed, was built, was added, was erected          |
| Removed     | was demolished, was removed, was knocked down, was replaced |
| Expanded    | was extended, was expanded, was enlarged, was widened       |
| Replaced    | was replaced by, was converted into, was transformed into   |
| Unchanged   | remained unchanged, was retained, stayed the same           |

### Verb Tenses

**Past Simple (completed change):**

> "A hospital **was built** in 2010."

**Present Perfect (change up to now):**

> "The town **has developed** significantly."

**Past Perfect (change before another):**

> "By 2000, the factory **had been demolished**."

### Location Prepositions

| Preposition                     | Use                            |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------ |
| to the north/south/east/west of | direction from reference point |

| on the corner of                | where two roads meet           |
| adjacent to / next to           | immediately beside             |
| opposite                        | facing                         |
| along                           | following the length of        |
| on the outskirts of             | on the edge of town            |
| surrounding                     | around                         |

## üìù Sample Map Answer

### Maps: Changes to Westbury from 1965 to 2015

**Introduction:**
The two maps compare the town of Westbury in 1965 and 2015, highlighting the changes that occurred over this 50-year period.

**Overview:**
Overall, Westbury has undergone substantial development, transforming from a primarily rural area to an urban residential zone. The most significant changes include the replacement of farmland with housing and the expansion of the road network.

**Body 1 (Western area):**
In 1965, the western side of Westbury was dominated by farmland. By 2015, this agricultural land had been completely replaced by a large housing estate. Additionally, the small path that previously crossed the farmland was widened and developed into a main road connecting the new residential area to the town center.

**Body 2 (Eastern area and other changes):**
On the eastern side, the woods that existed in 1965 have been partially cleared to make way for a shopping mall. However, a section of woodland was retained in the northeastern corner. The river running through the center of the town remained unchanged throughout the period. Furthermore, a new bridge was constructed over the river to improve access between the eastern and western areas.
